## Twitter / X (2 Threads)

### Thread 1: "Why 304 Stainless Steel Matters"

**Tweet 1:**
Most dough whisks say "stainless steel." But there are 150+ grades of stainless steel — and not all of them belong in your kitchen.

Here's why 304 stainless steel is the one you want. 🧵

**Tweet 2:**
304 stainless steel (also called 18/8) contains 18% chromium and 8% nickel. This combination creates:

• Resistance to corrosion and rust
• Higher ductility (won't bend under heavy dough)
• A self-healing oxide layer that protects the surface

**Tweet 3:**
Compare that to 430 stainless steel — the grade most budget whisks use:

• Less chromium = more prone to rust
• No nickel = more brittle, easier to bend
• Shorter lifespan under regular use

**Tweet 4:**
The math:
• $5 whisk (430 SS) replaced 3x/year = $15/year
• $10 whisk (304 SS) lasting 5+ years = $2/year

One good tool beats three cheap ones.

### Thread 2: "Sourdough Tool Mistakes"

**Tweet 1:**
5 mistakes every new sourdough baker makes (and how to fix them) 🧵

**Tweet 2:**
Mistake #1: Using a wooden spoon for high-hydration dough.

Wooden spoons clog. Dough sticks. You end up spending more time cleaning the spoon than mixing the loaf.

Fix: Get a Danish dough whisk with an open-coil design.

**Tweet 3:**
Mistake #2: Not knowing what grade of stainless steel your tools are made from.

"Stainless steel" can mean anything from 430 (cheap, bends easily) to 304 (professional-grade, resists corrosion).

Fix: Look for "304 stainless steel" specifically.

**Tweet 4:**
Mistake #3: Overmixing your dough.

High-hydration sourdough doesn't need aggressive mixing. A good dough whisk cuts through the dough without tearing the gluten structure.

Fix: Mix until just combined, then let time do the work.

**Tweet 5:**
Mistake #4: Skipping a kitchen scale.

Sourdough is measured in baker's percentages. Cups are imprecise. A $10 scale will transform your results.

**Tweet 6:**
Mistake #5: Buying too many tools before you need them.

You need: a whisk, a bowl, a scale, a Dutch oven, and a banneton. That's it. Start simple, add later.
