## FAQs

### Q: What's the difference between a Danish dough whisk and a regular whisk?
A: A Danish dough whisk has thick, looped stainless steel wires designed to cut through heavy doughs without getting stuck. A regular whisk is made for thin liquids and will bend or clog with thick bread dough. If you're making sourdough, you need a Danish whisk — it's the right tool for the job.

### Q: How many blades come with the bread lame?
A: The lame ships with replaceable razor blades installed. Replacement blades are standard double-edge razor blades — inexpensive and available at any pharmacy or online. You won't need to replace them often, but when you do, it's quick and cheap.

### Q: Is this set good for beginners who've never made bread?
A: Absolutely. This set is designed for people who are just starting their sourdough journey. The tools are simple to use, easy to clean, and built to grow with you as you improve. At $10.99, it's the lowest-risk way to try bread making without investing in a full kit.

### Q: Are the handles real wood?
A: Yes. Both the Danish whisk and the bread lame feature natural wooden handles with a smooth finish. They feel solid and comfortable in your hand — not cheap or plasticky. Hand wash only to preserve the wood.

### Q: Can I use this set for other types of bread besides sourdough?
A: Definitely. The Danish whisk works beautifully for any thick dough — pizza, focaccia, ciabatta, whole wheat, and more. The bread lame scores any bread type with clean, precise cuts. This isn't a single-use set — it's a bread-making essential.
