## Email 2: Education — "Why scoring matters"

**Send:** Day 2-3
**Subject Line:** Why the knife isn't cutting it (literally)
**Preview Text:** The scoring technique that changes everything

---

Hey [First Name],

Quick question: have you ever scored a sourdough loaf with a regular kitchen knife?

If so, you've probably noticed the dough drags, the cut isn't clean, and the loaf doesn't open up the way you see in those Instagram photos.

That's not your fault. That's a knife problem.

### Why scoring matters more than you think

When you score bread, you're telling the dough where to expand. Without a clean score, steam finds the weakest spot and blows out the side of your loaf. With a clean score, you get:

- A beautiful "ear" — that crispy ridge on top
- Even expansion for a better crumb
- Professional-looking results every time

The difference between a knife and a bread lame is the difference between dragging and slicing. A lame uses a razor-sharp blade that glides through dough with almost no effort.

### The tool that makes it easy

Our bread lame is designed for one thing: clean, precise scores on sourdough loaves. It fits comfortably in your hand, uses standard replaceable blades, and gives you the control you need to create patterns from simple to stunning.
